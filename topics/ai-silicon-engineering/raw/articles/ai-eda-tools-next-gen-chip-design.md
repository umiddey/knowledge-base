# AI-Driven EDA Tools: Automating Next-Gen Chip Design

## Why AI Is Essential
- Each new process node increases cost of mistakes (tape-out can cost tens of millions)
- Traditional tools rely on static algorithms, heuristics, manual oversight
- ML models analyze enormous datasets, find patterns humans cannot
- RL algorithms explore placement options at machine speed, often finding solutions that surprise veteran designers

## How AI Reshapes the Workflow

### Floorplanning & Routing
AI replaces heuristics with learned strategies that improve with every project. Proposes layouts already meeting timing, power, and density requirements.

### Verification
AI identifies which scenarios most likely to expose critical bugs. Reduces simulation time while increasing coverage.

### Synthesis
ML helps optimize designs for different manufacturing nodes, adapting automatically to quirks of each fabrication process.

### Manufacturing
Predictive models forecast yield issues before production begins by analyzing defect data from previous wafers.

## Industry Adoption
- RL models complete placement and routing in hours instead of months
- AI-generated layouts outperform expert-optimized ones in some cases
- EDA vendors integrating ML features directly into platforms
- Startups emerging for analog circuit design and mixed-signal systems
- Applications: aerospace (radiation-hardened), automotive (functional safety), neuromorphic/quantum research

## Challenges
- **Trust**: Engineers want transparency, AI is often a black box
- **Data availability**: Design/manufacturing data is proprietary
- **Scale**: Training large RL models requires massive compute
- **Human factor**: Shifts job roles toward guiding AI, validating results
