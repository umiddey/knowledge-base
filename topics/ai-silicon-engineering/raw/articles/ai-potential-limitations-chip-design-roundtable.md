# AI's Potential And Limitations In Chip Design — Expert Roundtable

Panelists: Synopsys, Intel, AMD, Nvidia, Microsoft, UC Berkeley

## Key Quotes

**Synopsys (Thomas Andersen)**: "AI will not replace the tools themselves. For the operator — the human who runs verification, implementation, signoff tools — there are a lot of iterations. That type of work will be highly automated in the next two years. Complete automation will take longer — human in the loop is important."

**AMD (Alex Starr)**: "Five weeks or five years? Things are moving so fast. Agentic flows are going to completely transform silicon design."

**Nvidia (Stuart Oberman)**: "Use AI to completely consume the task work. We see so much technology becoming available, we're trying to deploy it as fast as we can."

**Intel (Sridhar Boinapally)**: "This is a fundamental change for EDA. A lot of design is mundane work in verification. All of that is gone. It may even take over all of chip design."

**Microsoft (Silvian Goldenberg)**: "We have to get it right from day one. That's how chip design works. AI has the ability to shape the software and the implementation for a more optimal solution."

**UC Berkeley (Borivoje Nikolic)**: "AI has discovered a new cache replacement policy in general-purpose cores that beats human development. We also built an AI discovery flow to develop analog circuits — it invented topologies that had been seen before but not used because they're hard to reason about."

## On Full Autonomy
**AMD**: "We will get to fully autonomous. Collections of agents working on things together. From coding today to enterprise-grade, fully scalable, deterministic solutions."

## On Differentiation
**Nvidia**: "Being able to codify institutional expertise and embed it into models and frameworks — that will continue. Some will succeed more than others."
