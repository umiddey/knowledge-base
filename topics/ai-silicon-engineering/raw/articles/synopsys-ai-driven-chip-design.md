# AI-Driven Chip Design (Synopsys)

AI-driven chip design uses AI technologies (reinforcement learning, generative AI, AI agents) in the tool flow to design, verify, and test semiconductor devices.

## How It Works

### Reinforcement Learning
- Learns optimal behavior via interactions with environment
- Well-suited to EDA workloads — analyzes complex problems holistically
- Used in chip designs for over 1,000 tapeouts across the industry
- Can be considered proven technology

### Generative AI
- LLM-based copilots assist engineers in the design process
- Used for initial code generation, documentation lookup, ramping up junior engineers

### AI Agents
- Automate mundane/repetitive tasks
- L1 to L5 capability stages, ultimately full autonomy for allowed decisions

## Benefits
- **Enhanced PPA**: AI explores large design spaces to find optimal power/performance/area
- **Enhanced Productivity**: Frees engineers from iterative tasks
- **Support for Reuse**: Learnings retained across projects
- **Faster Design Migration**: Quicker node-to-node migration

## Key Distinction
- **AI chip design**: Building specialized hardware that powers AI (GPUs, NPUs, ASICs)
- **AI-driven chip design**: Using AI within the chip development process itself
