# AI Chip Alternatives to Nvidia for Startups

## Tenstorrent Grayskull
Open source AI chips gaining momentum as developers demand transparency. Jim Keller's vision for open-source RISC-V based AI silicon.

## Groq LPU
Language Processing Unit — deterministic, low-latency inference. Extremely fast for specific workloads but questions about scalability and cost.

## Cerebras WSE
Wafer-scale engine — entire wafer as single processor. Eliminates chip-to-chip communication overhead for massive models.

## SambaNova DataScale
Reconfigurable dataflow architecture. CGRA-based approach for both training and inference.

## AMD MI300 Series
Competitive HBM capacity and bandwidth. Gaining traction with cost-conscious customers.

## Google TPU (Cloud)
Available via Google Cloud. Custom-designed for TensorFlow and JAX workloads.

## AWS Trainium/Inferentia
Amazon's custom silicon for training (Trainium) and inference (Inferentia). Deep integration with AWS ecosystem.

## Key Theme
The move from GPU monoculture to diverse, domain-specific silicon is accelerating. Open-source hardware (Tenstorrent) and cloud-native custom silicon (Trainium, TPU) represent two different disruption vectors against Nvidia's dominance.
