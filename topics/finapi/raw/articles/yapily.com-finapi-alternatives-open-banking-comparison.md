# finAPI Alternatives: Open Banking Comparison

*Source: Yapily blog (competitor perspective, accurate as of Nov 2025)*

## finAPI's Position

- **Coverage**: 13 European markets, main focus on Germany and Austria
- **Strengths**: German market depth, BaFin-licensed PIS+AIS, Web Form 2.0 for non-licensed integrators, data intelligence (categorisation, credit checks)
- **Model**: White-label + hosted web form options

## Key Competitors

| Provider | Banks | Countries | Specialty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yapily | 2,000+ | 19 EU + UK | DACH + UK scale, AIS+PIS depth |
| TrueLayer | Wide EU | 18+ | FCA-regulated, UK focus |
| Tink (Visa) | 3,400+ | 18 EU | Swedish origin, acquired by Visa 2022 |
| Salt Edge | ~5,000 | 44 global | Broadest geographic coverage |
| Token | EU+UK | EU | giroAPI in Germany, micropayments without SCA |
| BANKSapi | Germany/DACH | DACH | EBICS + non-EBICS, German corporates |

## Why finAPI is Chosen (Over Alternatives)

- Deepest German bank connectivity
- BaFin PIS license means no PSD2 license required from integrating company
- Web Form 2.0 handles all SCA complexity automatically
- Data intelligence products (beyond just payment initiation)
- Established since early German open banking

## finAPI Limitations vs Alternatives

- 13 markets vs Yapily's 19, Tink's 18, Salt Edge's 44
- No strong UK presence (Yapily, TrueLayer stronger there)
- Less suited for companies needing pan-European or global coverage without multiple providers

## Industry Context

PSD2 mandated open banking APIs across EU since 2018. Berlin Group NextGenPSD2 became the dominant technical standard in Germany/DACH. finAPI built its platform on this standard, giving it deep DACH penetration but limiting broad international reach compared to providers who launched with a broader mandate.
