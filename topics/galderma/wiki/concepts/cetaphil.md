# Cetaphil

Cetaphil is Galderma’s flagship consumer-sensitive-skin brand. The company traces it back to 1947, says it has been recommended by dermatologists for more than 70 years, and says it is available in more than 70 countries. The brand covers cleansers, moisturizers, sun protection, baby products, and solutions for acne, eczema, and rosacea. In practice, that makes Cetaphil the most mass-market part of Galderma’s portfolio.

Cetaphil matters because it gives Galderma repeatable consumer volume rather than only clinic-based or prescription-based sales. The brand sits at the intersection of everyday skincare and medical credibility. In the company’s own reporting, Cetaphil is one of the key drivers of the Dermatological Skincare segment and a major contributor to the segment crossing the $1 billion mark in annual sales.

The brand also plays a strategic role beyond volume. It gives Galderma a broad consumer channel, an e-commerce footprint, and a recognizable dermatologist recommendation story that can be extended into new lines and markets. That makes Cetaphil less like a niche premium SKU and more like a platform for recurring skincare revenue.

## Key Points
- Cetaphil began in 1947 and has a 70+ year dermatologist-recommendation story.
- The brand is sold in more than 70 countries.
- It spans daily cleansing, moisturizing, sun protection, and specialty sensitive-skin products.
- Galderma positions Cetaphil as a major growth driver inside Dermatological Skincare.

## Example
A person with sensitive skin may buy Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser, Cetaphil moisturizer, and Cetaphil sunscreen as a daily routine. That is a very different purchase pattern from a one-time clinic procedure, which is why Cetaphil is so valuable to Galderma.
