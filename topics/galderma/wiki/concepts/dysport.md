# Dysport

Dysport is Galderma’s core botulinum toxin type A brand and one of the foundational assets in Injectable Aesthetics. It is used for aesthetic indications, especially temporary improvement of moderate to severe glabellar lines. In Europe, the aesthetic version is marketed as Azzalure. Dysport remains important because it gives Galderma a legacy neuromodulator franchise with broad physician familiarity.

Dysport is strategically important even in a portfolio that now includes newer products like Alluzience and Relfydess. It gives Galderma installed base, brand recognition, and clinical trust. The newer ready-to-use liquid products are not replacing Dysport; they are extending the company’s toxin portfolio into adjacent workflow and convenience positions.

The practical value of Dysport is easy to understand: a clinician wants a reliable neuromodulator for facial expression lines. Dysport is the established answer inside Galderma’s lineup, and it sits at the start of a broader aesthetic treatment conversation that can later lead to fillers or biostimulators.

## Key Points
- Dysport is a botulinum toxin type A product for aesthetic use.
- It is a central brand in Galderma’s neuromodulator franchise.
- The EU aesthetic version is called Azzalure.
- Dysport provides the legacy anchor for newer toxin launches.

## Example
A patient with frown lines might receive Dysport at a dermatology office for glabellar line reduction. If the same patient later wants lower-face refinement, the clinician can add Restylane or Sculptra from the same company.
