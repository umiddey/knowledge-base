# Galderma Overview

Galderma is a pure-play dermatology company built around three revenue engines: Injectable Aesthetics, Dermatological Skincare, and Therapeutic Dermatology. The company’s own reporting shows that this is not a loose brand collection. It is a deliberately integrated portfolio aimed at the full dermatology lifecycle, from consumer skincare to clinician-administered injectables to prescription treatments for chronic skin disease. In 2025, Galderma reported net sales of $5.207 billion, with Injectable Aesthetics the largest segment, followed by Dermatological Skincare and Therapeutic Dermatology.

The modern Galderma story starts with its 2024 IPO on the SIX Swiss Exchange, which reset the company as a public dermatology platform with global scale. Since then, management has emphasized growth through premium brands, regulatory approvals, and international rollout. The portfolio is broad enough that a patient can encounter Galderma in several different contexts: Cetaphil for daily sensitive-skin care, Dysport or Restylane for aesthetics, and Nemluvio or AKLIEF for prescription disease treatment.

Galderma’s business model matters because it is not just selling one category of product. It is using the same dermatology relationships, scientific framing, and clinic channels to expand across multiple adjacent demand pools. That is why strategic investors such as L’Oréal matter too: the company sits at the boundary of beauty, medical dermatology, and regulated therapeutics.

The latest 2025 annual report, published in 2026, sharpened that picture further. Galderma said it is present in approximately 90 countries and now describes itself as the "undisputed dermatology powerhouse." It also gave 2026 guidance for 17-20% constant-currency net sales growth and about a 26% Core EBITDA margin, which is strong for a company that is already above $5 billion in annual sales. That combination of scale and growth is the real story here.

## Key Points
- Galderma described 2025 net sales of $5.207 billion, up 17.7% at constant currency.
- Injectable Aesthetics generated $2.572 billion in 2025, or about half of company revenue.
- Dermatological Skincare contributed $1.449 billion, while Therapeutic Dermatology contributed $1.185 billion.
- The 2024 IPO was a structural turning point, not just a financing event.
- Galderma’s portfolio spans consumer skincare, physician-dispensed skincare, injectables, and prescription drugs.
- The company now operates in roughly 90 countries and is guiding for continued double-digit growth.

## Example
A dermatologist’s practice might use Cetaphil for daily sensitive-skin maintenance, ALASTIN after procedures, Dysport for glabellar lines, Restylane Lyft for chin profile, and Nemluvio for a patient with prurigo nodularis. The patient sees separate products; Galderma sees one integrated dermatology platform.
