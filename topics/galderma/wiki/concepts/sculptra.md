# Sculptra

Sculptra is Galderma’s collagen-stimulating biostimulator, which makes it different from both neuromodulators and standard hyaluronic-acid fillers. Galderma describes it as a unique aesthetic treatment that activates the skin’s natural collagen production for a more youthful appearance. The product has a long history, with the company describing first approval in Europe in 1999 and availability in more than 40 countries.

The commercial point of Sculptra is durability and regenerative effect. Galderma’s 2025 materials emphasize its role in medication-driven weight-loss facial changes and in cheek wrinkles, and its China launch shows the company wants this premium product in fast-growing aesthetics markets. Sculptra is especially useful where the goal is longer-lasting volume restoration rather than immediate but temporary fill.

Because it stimulates collagen, Sculptra occupies a different psychological and clinical niche from Restylane. A patient choosing Sculptra is often looking for slower, more structural improvement. That makes the product a strong complement to the rest of Galderma’s injectables stack.

## Key Points
- Sculptra is a collagen-stimulating biostimulator, not a standard filler.
- The brand has long commercial history and global reach.
- Cheek wrinkle and China approvals expanded its market.
- Galderma uses Sculptra in the weight-loss-face and regenerative-aesthetics narrative.

## Example
A patient who lost a lot of weight and wants gradual restoration of facial volume may choose Sculptra rather than a traditional HA filler. The appeal is longer-lasting, collagen-driven improvement rather than a purely volumizing gel effect.
