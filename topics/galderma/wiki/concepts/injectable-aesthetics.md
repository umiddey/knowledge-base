# Injectable Aesthetics

Injectable Aesthetics is Galderma’s largest segment and the clearest source of near-term revenue power. In 2025, the segment generated $2.572 billion, or roughly 49% of total sales. The segment is built on three product families: neuromodulators, hyaluronic-acid fillers, and biostimulators. That mix matters because it gives Galderma a broader toolkit than a single “Botox competitor” story.

The portfolio includes Dysport and Alluzience in the neuromodulator category, Restylane for hyaluronic-acid fillers and Skinboosters, and Sculptra for collagen stimulation. Relfydess is the newer ready-to-use liquid neuromodulator that Galderma positions as a workflow-improving innovation. The company’s label expansions, such as Restylane Lyft for chin profile and Sculptra for cheek wrinkles, show that much of the segment’s growth comes from extending existing brands into new anatomical or functional uses.

Commercially, the segment benefits from repeat demand, high procedure economics, and patient preference for visible but natural-looking results. The “Ozempic face” theme matters here because GLP-1 weight loss creates a new aesthetic use case for fillers and biostimulators. That gives Galderma a tailwind that is separate from standard anti-aging demand.

The 2025 annual report and IMCAS 2025 release also show how Galderma is modernizing the segment without abandoning the legacy portfolio. Relfydess was described as a ready-to-use neuromodulator with up to 39% of patients seeing effects from day one and up to 75% maintaining improvements for six months. The company also highlighted that the product does not require reconstitution, which matters in a real clinic because workflow and dose consistency affect throughput.

## Key Points
- Injectable Aesthetics was $2.572 billion in 2025 sales.
- Neuromodulators and fillers/biostimulators both contribute meaningfully.
- Relfydess, Restylane Lyft, and Sculptra are all examples of portfolio expansion.
- Galderma markets the segment around clinician workflow, natural-looking results, and broad facial coverage.
- Workflow convenience is now a meaningful source of differentiation, not just a side benefit.

## Example
An aesthetic patient with forehead lines, lower-face volume loss, and chin retrusion could receive Dysport for wrinkles, Restylane Lyft for chin profile, and Sculptra for longer-lasting volume support. That one treatment plan could touch three Galderma product families.
