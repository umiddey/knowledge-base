# GLP-1 Aesthetics Demand

GLP-1-driven weight loss created a new aesthetic demand pattern that Galderma can serve with fillers, biostimulators, and regenerative skincare. The market shorthand for this is “Ozempic face,” which refers to facial volume loss and more visible aging after rapid weight reduction. In Galderma’s case, the opportunity is especially relevant to Restylane, Sculptra, and ALASTIN.

The important part is not the slang itself but the treatment behavior it implies. Patients who lose weight quickly may want lower-face volume restoration, chin refinement, or skin-quality support. That can push demand toward Restylane Lyft, Sculptra, and supporting skincare products. Galderma’s aesthetics and skincare teams have both leaned into this trend in public messaging and product launches.

This is a good example of how external health trends can create adjacent demand for a dermatology company. It is not a core disease franchise like Nemluvio, but it is a meaningful demand tailwind for the aesthetics business.

## Key Points
- “Ozempic face” is a market term for facial volume loss after GLP-1 weight loss.
- The demand tailwind favors fillers, biostimulators, and regenerative skincare.
- Restylane, Sculptra, and ALASTIN are the most directly relevant Galderma assets.
- The theme links consumer health trends to aesthetics revenue.

## Example
A patient who loses 40 pounds on a GLP-1 drug may ask for chin refinement and midface volume restoration. Galderma’s response could be Restylane Lyft for structure, Sculptra for longer-term collagen support, and ALASTIN for peri-procedural skin care.
