# Integrated Dermatology Strategy

Galderma’s integrated dermatology strategy is the logic that ties its portfolio together. Instead of treating each brand as a standalone asset, the company uses shared dermatology relationships, complementary products, and repeated patient touchpoints to create commercial synergies. That shows up most clearly in clinics: the same aesthetic practice can buy injectables, regenerative skincare, and prescription dermatology products from the same supplier.

The strategy is visible in the way Galderma talks about its assets. ALASTIN is described as a physician-dispensed skincare brand that fits with the aesthetics portfolio. Relfydess is positioned as a portfolio-expanding neuromodulator. Nemluvio is framed as a growth driver in a disease area where Galderma already sells multiple products. This is a classic “platform” model: each new brand can piggyback on the existing medical, commercial, and regulatory infrastructure.

The advantage is reach and repetition. A patient may only get a filler once or twice a year, but skincare and prescription therapies create more recurring engagement. That gives Galderma more opportunities to keep the customer inside the same commercial ecosystem, rather than forcing the practice or patient to buy from multiple vendors.

Galderma’s 2025 materials make the operating system more concrete. Cetaphil became a digital-first growth engine with e-commerce as its fastest-growing channel, while ALASTIN was pushed through physician-first engagement and clinic-partnership programs. On the therapeutics side, Nemluvio’s strong launch trajectory showed that the same company can also drive a biologic through broad education, market access, and field-force expansion. The strategy is not just portfolio breadth; it is a repeatable commercial engine.

## Key Points
- Galderma says its model is “integrated dermatology,” not a loose set of brands.
- The strategy relies on shared HCP relationships and adjacent product categories.
- ALASTIN, Cetaphil, Dysport, Restylane, Sculptra, and Nemluvio all reinforce one another commercially.
- The model helps explain why Galderma emphasizes innovation, education, and practice partnerships.

## Example
A medical aesthetics practice might use ALASTIN to support pre- and post-procedure skin care, Dysport for expression lines, Restylane or Sculptra for volume, and then recommend Cetaphil for daily maintenance. That sequence keeps the patient and the practice inside Galderma’s ecosystem across multiple visits.
