# ALASTIN

ALASTIN is Galderma’s physician-dispensed skincare brand and the professional-side counterpart to Cetaphil. Galderma completed the acquisition of ALASTIN in January 2022 and described it as a specialty aesthetics company focused on innovative, clinically tested skincare. That acquisition gave Galderma a stronger presence in peri-procedural and regenerative skincare, where clinics want products that support recovery, skin quality, and treatment planning.

ALASTIN’s role in the portfolio is not static. Galderma launched ALASTIN Signature Practices in 2026, a program that designates clinics that consistently integrate ALASTIN into patient care. The brand also expanded internationally, including a China launch in 2025, and introduced new products such as Restorative Skin Complex. This shows ALASTIN is being used as both a product line and a clinic-adoption platform.

The commercial logic is straightforward: ALASTIN sits close to the aesthetics workflow. If a clinic is already using Galderma injectables, ALASTIN gives the company a way to sell the supporting skincare around the procedure. That makes ALASTIN strategically important well beyond its direct sales contribution.

Galderma’s 2026 ALASTIN Signature Practices launch makes that logic even clearer. The first practice was in Colorado Springs, and the program uses curated product displays, ongoing product education, co-marketing, and dedicated support to strengthen patient engagement. This is not just a brand badge; it is a clinic loyalty system built around peri-procedural skincare.

## Key Points
- ALASTIN joined Galderma through a 2022 acquisition.
- It is a physician-dispensed, peri-procedural, and regenerative skincare brand.
- Signature Practices and international launches show active channel expansion.
- ALASTIN complements injectables by owning the skin-care layer around procedures.
- The 2026 Signature Practices program shows that ALASTIN is being used as a clinic operating layer, not only a product line.

## Example
A clinic may recommend ALASTIN Restorative Skin Complex before and after a laser or injectable treatment to support skin quality and recovery. That keeps the patient inside Galderma’s ecosystem even when they are not receiving an injection.
