# Therapeutic Dermatology

Therapeutic Dermatology is Galderma’s prescription business for chronic and clinically significant skin conditions. In 2025 the segment generated $1.185 billion, or about 23% of sales, and it was the fastest-growing segment in the portfolio. The segment includes biologics, prescription acne drugs, rosacea treatments, pigmentary-disorder products, and skin-cancer-related therapies.

The key growth story is Nemluvio, Galderma’s nemolizumab asset for prurigo nodularis and atopic dermatitis. But the segment is broader than that. Acne products such as AKLIEF, Differin, and TWYNEO, rosacea products such as Soolantra and EPSOLAY, and niche products like Tri-Luma all sit under the same commercial umbrella. That breadth matters because it gives Galderma multiple disease-area franchises rather than a single drug dependency.

Therapeutic Dermatology is where Galderma combines regulatory execution with medical need. This segment does not depend on consumer whim; it depends on approvals, efficacy, and physician adoption. That makes it slower than injectables, but it also creates durable franchises once products are established.

## Key Points
- Therapeutic Dermatology reached $1.185 billion in 2025 sales.
- Nemluvio is the main growth driver and a potential blockbuster asset.
- Acne, rosacea, and pigmentary-disorder treatments make the segment broader and more resilient.
- The business relies on regulatory approvals and physician adoption rather than consumer marketing alone.

## Example
A patient with severe prurigo nodularis may start Nemluvio, a teen with acne may begin on Differin or TWYNEO, and a rosacea patient may use Soolantra or EPSOLAY. The common thread is that Galderma is addressing chronic dermatology problems with different tools across the same commercial segment.
