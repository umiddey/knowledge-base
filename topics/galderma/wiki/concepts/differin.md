# Differin

Differin is Galderma’s consumer acne brand built around adapalene, a topical retinoid that moved from prescription strength into wider consumer access. That transition matters because it shows how Galderma uses regulatory milestones to expand an acne brand’s reach rather than relying only on new molecule launches. In Galderma’s portfolio, Differin sits on the consumer side of the acne ladder and helps pull patients into the company’s broader dermatology ecosystem.

The brand matters strategically because acne is one of the clearest “step-up” disease areas in Galderma’s portfolio. A patient can start with Differin at the OTC level and later move to stronger prescription options like AKLIEF or TWYNEO if acne remains uncontrolled. That makes Differin a high-volume entry point, not just a standalone SKU.

Differin is also a useful example of how Galderma monetizes dermatology knowledge at scale. The brand turns a clinically credible active ingredient into mass-market access, which helps the company participate in both consumer and medical acne demand.

## Key Points
- Differin is the consumer/OTC acne anchor in Galderma’s portfolio.
- The brand was originally prescription strength and later moved to broader consumer access.
- It sits at the low-intensity end of Galderma’s acne escalation pathway.
- Differin helps connect consumer skincare to prescription dermatology.

## Example
A teen with mild acne might begin with Differin Gel at home. If the acne does not respond, a dermatologist can step the patient up to AKLIEF or TWYNEO, keeping the patient inside Galderma’s acne franchise.
