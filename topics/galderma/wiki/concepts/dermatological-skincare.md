# Dermatological Skincare

Dermatological Skincare is Galderma’s bridge between consumer beauty and medical dermatology. In 2025 the segment generated $1.449 billion, about 28% of company sales. The segment is anchored by Cetaphil, the sensitive-skin consumer brand, and ALASTIN, the physician-dispensed professional skincare brand. Together they give Galderma both mass-market reach and clinic-level influence.

Cetaphil has a long history as a dermatologist-recommended daily skincare brand, while ALASTIN is more directly tied to aesthetic procedures and regenerative skincare. That combination is commercially useful: Cetaphil captures recurring household use, while ALASTIN can be sold through clinics around procedures, recovery, and skin quality. The company also keeps expanding both brands internationally and across channels, including e-commerce and practice-based programs.

This segment matters because skincare is more recurring than injectables and easier to scale across consumers. It also gives Galderma a way to participate in aesthetic demand without relying only on procedures. In a business sense, Dermatological Skincare is the repeat-purchase layer of the portfolio.

The company’s 2025 results made the segment’s operating model more explicit. Cetaphil’s digital-first strategy turned e-commerce into its fastest-growing channel, with major activations in China and strong global brand reach. ALASTIN, meanwhile, was pushed through physician-first engagement and clinic-based programs such as Signature Practices. That means the segment spans both consumer commerce and clinic workflow, which is exactly what Galderma wants.

## Key Points
- Dermatological Skincare generated $1.449 billion in 2025 sales.
- Cetaphil is the core consumer brand.
- ALASTIN is the professional, physician-dispensed brand.
- E-commerce, retail, and clinic partnership programs are all important to the segment.
- The segment combines consumer digital growth with physician-led professional adoption.

## Example
A patient might use Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser every day, then add ALASTIN Restorative Skin Complex after a laser or injectable procedure. The same company then captures both the daily routine and the post-procedure regimen.
