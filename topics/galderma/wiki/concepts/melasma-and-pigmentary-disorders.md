# Melasma and Pigmentary Disorders

Melasma and pigmentary disorders are a niche but durable part of Galderma’s prescription dermatology portfolio. The anchor product here is Tri-Luma, which Galderma described as the only FDA-approved treatment for moderate-to-severe facial melasma at the time of its launch communication. Tri-Luma combines a corticosteroid, a depigmenting agent, and a retinoid, making it a classic example of combination dermatology therapy.

This area matters because it shows the breadth of Galderma’s prescription franchise beyond acne and rosacea. Melasma is not the largest commercial category in the portfolio, but it is clinically important and persistent. That gives Galderma a durable niche in a condition that many patients care about and that often requires specialist management.

The product also reinforces a recurring theme in Galderma’s prescription business: layering active ingredients to meet a specific clinical need. That combination approach shows up in other parts of the portfolio as well, but Tri-Luma is one of the cleanest examples.

## Key Points
- Tri-Luma is the core melasma product.
- The indication is short-term treatment of moderate-to-severe facial melasma.
- The product combines three actives in one cream.
- The niche is small but clinically durable.

## Example
A patient with facial melasma may need short-term combination therapy plus sun avoidance measures. Tri-Luma is designed for that use case, not for everyday moisturizer-style skincare.
