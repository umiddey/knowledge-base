# Restylane

Restylane is Galderma’s hyaluronic-acid injectable family, and one of the most commercially important assets in Injectable Aesthetics. Galderma describes it as a range that includes fillers and Skinboosters. The franchise is built on a long clinical history and a broad portfolio of facial-use indications, which makes it more than a single product line. In 2025, Galderma said the franchise had delivered more than 77 million treatments worldwide.

The Restylane family matters because it covers a broad range of aesthetic use cases: facial folds, cheek volume, hands, chin profile, and skin quality. The Restylane Lyft chin-profile approval is a good example of the label-expansion model Galderma uses to keep the franchise fresh and clinically relevant. The company also uses Restylane to talk about natural-looking results and facial-expression preservation.

This family is important to Galderma’s economics because hyaluronic-acid fillers often sit in the middle of the aesthetic treatment journey. They are more durable than skincare, more structural than neuromodulators, and often complementary to Sculptra or toxin products. That makes Restylane a central driver of repeat aesthetic revenue.

## Key Points
- Restylane is a hyaluronic-acid filler and Skinbooster family.
- Galderma says the franchise has delivered over 77 million treatments worldwide.
- The line spans chin profile, facial folds, cheeks, hands, and skin quality.
- Label expansion is a core growth tactic for the franchise.

## Example
A patient who wants a stronger chin profile and smoother midface might receive Restylane Lyft for structural enhancement. If they later want skin-quality improvement, the clinician could add Restylane Skinboosters.
