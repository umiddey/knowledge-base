# Dermatology Commercial Ladder

Galderma’s commercial model runs from consumer skincare to physician-dispensed skincare to injectables. Cetaphil is the daily consumer entry point, ALASTIN is the clinic-adjacent professional skincare layer, and Injectable Aesthetics is the high-value procedure layer. This is the cleanest way to see how the company builds repeat purchase behavior across the dermatology lifecycle.

## Example
A patient may use Cetaphil at home, ALASTIN around a procedure, and then return to the clinic for Dysport, Restylane, or Sculptra. From Galderma’s point of view, that is one ladder with several monetization points.

## Analysis
The ladder matters because it explains why Galderma invests in both mass-market and clinic-based skincare. The company is not choosing between consumer and procedural markets; it is trying to connect them. That is a stronger business model than a one-channel portfolio because it creates more touchpoints and more chances to sell again.
