# Injectable Aesthetics Product Ladder

Galderma’s injectable aesthetics business is best understood as a ladder rather than a set of disconnected brands. Dysport, Alluzience, and Relfydess cover neuromodulators at different points on the convenience and modernization spectrum, while Restylane and Sculptra cover structural volume restoration and biostimulation. The commercial point is that Galderma can meet different clinic preferences without collapsing the franchise into a single product category.

## Example
A clinic might start with Dysport as the established toxin, move to Alluzience or Relfydess for a ready-to-use workflow advantage, then pair that toxin treatment with Restylane for immediate volume and Sculptra for longer-term collagen stimulation.

## Analysis
The ladder matters because it reduces single-brand dependency and lets Galderma segment the market by convenience, duration, and treatment goal. This is more flexible than a pure “Botox competitor” model. It also explains why label expansions and workflow innovations are so important in aesthetics: they create places for the same patient and clinic to buy again.
