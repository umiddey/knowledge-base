# Integrated Dermatology Platform

Galderma’s strategy only makes sense when you look at the portfolio as a system. The company is not just selling separate beauty, skincare, and drug brands. It is building one dermatology platform that spans consumer routines, clinic-based procedures, and prescription treatment. That is why the same company can sell Cetaphil, ALASTIN, Dysport, Restylane, Sculptra, and Nemluvio without that looking like a random conglomerate.

## Example
A clinic can recommend Cetaphil for daily skin care, ALASTIN for peri-procedural recovery, Restylane or Sculptra for aesthetic correction, and Nemluvio for a chronic inflammatory skin disease. Each product sits in a different category, but the commercial relationship is unified.

## Analysis
The value of the platform is commercial adjacency. Galderma can keep the same dermatologist, aesthetic injector, or patient relationship across multiple buying occasions. That matters because recurring skincare and prescription use can stabilize what would otherwise be a more cyclical injectables business. It also gives Galderma more surface area for innovation and label expansion.
