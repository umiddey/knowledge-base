# Skincare From Consumer To Clinic

Galderma’s skincare portfolio is not split cleanly between “mass market” and “medical.” Cetaphil and ALASTIN occupy different positions on the same spectrum. Cetaphil is the daily consumer-sensitive-skin brand, while ALASTIN is the physician-dispensed peri-procedural brand. Together they let Galderma own both the at-home routine and the clinic-adjacent regimen.

## Example
A patient may wash with Cetaphil every morning, then use ALASTIN Restorative Skin Complex after a laser session or injectable treatment. From Galderma’s perspective, that is one customer journey, not two separate markets.

## Analysis
The strategic value here is that Galderma can use consumer trust to widen the top of funnel and clinic credibility to deepen the bottom of funnel. Cetaphil gives the company reach; ALASTIN gives it higher-intensity professional engagement. That makes Dermatological Skincare more resilient than a single-channel business would be.
