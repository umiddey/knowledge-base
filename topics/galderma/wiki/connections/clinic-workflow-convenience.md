# Clinic Workflow Convenience

Galderma’s aesthetics and professional-skincare businesses increasingly compete on workflow. Dysport is the legacy toxin, Alluzience and Relfydess reduce or eliminate reconstitution, and ALASTIN Signature Practices wraps skincare adoption into a clinic support program. The common thread is that Galderma is trying to make the clinic’s day easier, faster, and more predictable.

## Example
A high-volume aesthetic practice may prefer a ready-to-use toxin such as Relfydess, then reinforce that same patient’s journey with ALASTIN products before and after treatment. The clinic reduces prep friction while Galderma increases the number of products on the same shelf.

## Analysis
Convenience is not a superficial feature in this business. It affects throughput, consistency, training burden, and the likelihood that a clinic will repeatedly reorder. Galderma’s product design and clinic programs increasingly target that operational layer, which is why neuromodulator innovation and professional-skincare programs are strategically linked.
