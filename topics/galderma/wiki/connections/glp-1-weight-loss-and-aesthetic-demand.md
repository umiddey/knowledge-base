# GLP-1 Weight Loss and Aesthetic Demand

Rapid GLP-1-driven weight loss created a new category of aesthetic demand around facial volume loss, skin laxity, and a more aged appearance. That is what the “Ozempic face” conversation is really about. For Galderma, this is not a side story. It is a demand tailwind for Restylane, Sculptra, and ALASTIN.

## Example
A patient on a GLP-1 medication loses enough weight that their cheeks flatten and their chin profile looks weaker. A Galderma-aligned treatment plan could use Restylane Lyft for structure, Sculptra for longer-term volume support, and ALASTIN for recovery and skin-quality maintenance.

## Analysis
This connection matters because it shows how an external health trend can create a new aesthetics use case without requiring a new drug. Galderma’s opportunity is strongest when the company can translate a cultural or medical trend into a clinic workflow that already exists. The company’s messaging around medication-driven weight loss is a good example of commercial agility in aesthetics.
