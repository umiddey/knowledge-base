# Source Notes
- Galderma frames itself as a pure-play dermatology company and describes 2025 as a year of strong momentum after its 2024 IPO.
- The report centers the company on three businesses: Injectable Aesthetics, Dermatological Skincare, and Therapeutic Dermatology.
- Management says the portfolio is science-led and intended to cover the full spectrum of dermatology.
- The report highlights Nemluvio approvals in the EU and other markets, plus FDA approval for Restylane Lyft for chin profile enhancement.
- It also says Relfydess and new Restylane and Sculptra indications were key Injectable Aesthetics approvals in 2025.
- Galderma says Cetaphil and Alastin are the flagship skincare brands inside Dermatological Skincare.
- The report emphasizes R&D, healthcare-professional education, and ESG as part of the growth model.
- It states the company operates in approximately 90 countries.
