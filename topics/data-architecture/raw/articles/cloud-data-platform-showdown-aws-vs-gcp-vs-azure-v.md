## The Problem With Cloud Platform Debates

Every data team eventually asks: _“Which cloud platform should we build on?”_  
And every LinkedIn thought leader eventually replies: _“It depends.”_

Here’s the hard truth: every major cloud data platform is powerful, flawed, and probably overkill for your use case. But knowing the strengths and weaknesses of each one helps you make a smarter choice — or at least complain about your current choice with more precision.

This is my brutally honest breakdown of the top contenders in 2025. No sales decks. No hype cycles. Just what you need to know.

## Where Each Platform Actually Wins

**BigQuery:**

*   Marketing & product analytics
*   ML prototyping with BQML
*   Companies that live in Google Cloud

**Snowflake:**

*   Centralized BI warehouse with lots of dashboards
*   Cross-cloud architecture
*   SaaS companies sharing data externally

**Databricks:**

*   Machine learning-heavy teams
*   Data scientists who code first, model second
*   Lakehouse architectures blending batch + stream

**Fabric:**

*   Azure shops needing a one-stop data platform
*   Power BI-first organizations
*   Early adopters of semantic modeling + AI copilots

**Redshift:**

*   AWS-centric orgs who want decent SQL without retooling
*   Teams with simple batch workloads

**Synapse:**

*   Azure-first teams with ETL-heavy pipelines
*   Shops already deep in the Microsoft ecosystem

## The Hard Truth: You’ll Probably Use 2 or 3 of These Anyway

Most companies don’t live in a single platform anymore. Your BI layer might be on Power BI, your ML pipelines on Databricks, and your product analytics in BigQuery.

Vendor sales decks show clean diagrams. Reality looks like a spaghetti chart taped to your wall.

## My Take: How to Actually Choose

*   Start with what your team knows. Cloud data stacks aren’t plug-and-play.
*   Pick what integrates best with your existing cloud provider.
*   Stop chasing hype. Choose the platform that solves your actual data problems today — not the buzzwords on next year’s roadmap.

And remember: no matter what you pick, **you still need clean data, good models, and a team that knows how to use them.**

The platform is just the beginning.

Now go forth and build your stack.  
(And if your dashboards still break, at least you’ll know which vendor to blame first.)
