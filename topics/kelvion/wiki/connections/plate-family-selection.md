# Plate Family Selection

Kelvion’s plate portfolio is really three different operating philosophies wrapped in the same geometry. Brazed plates win when compactness and fast manufacturing matter; gasketed plates win when serviceability and future reconfiguration matter; welded plates win when pressure, temperature, or gas-tight operation dominates the spec. The family page makes this explicit, and the product pages add the practical envelope: drinking-water certification for some brazed units, semi-welded high-pressure heat-pump duty for gasketed HPL units, and `K°Bloc` for high-duty welded service.

This is why the family is not interchangeable. A heat pump installer and a refinery engineer may both ask for a “plate heat exchanger,” but they are solving different problems. One wants a small unit that can be produced quickly; the other wants a unit that survives severe duty without leaks or frequent service. Kelvion’s portfolio is built around that decision tree.

## Example
A propane heat-pump developer working with Fraunhofer on a low-charge circuit would likely start with a brazed plate exchanger because low fill volume and compactness matter. A district-heating operator, by contrast, would be happier with a gasketed plate exchanger because serviceability and expansion are more valuable over the asset life.

## Analysis
The key design axis is lifecycle control. Brazed units optimize for manufacturing simplicity and footprint. Gasketed units optimize for maintainability and configurability. Welded units optimize for containment and severe-duty operation. That tradeoff structure explains why Kelvion keeps all three variants in the portfolio instead of collapsing everything into one standard platform.
