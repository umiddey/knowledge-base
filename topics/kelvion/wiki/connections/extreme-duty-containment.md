# Extreme-Duty Containment

When the duty envelope becomes extreme, Kelvion shifts away from modularity and toward barriers, bonded interfaces, and leak detection. The shell-and-tube double-safety family uses dual walls and a monitored leakage space. `K°Bond` pushes into diffusion-bonded, leakage-free operation at up to `1,050 bar` and `600°C`. The carbon-capture and hydrogen pages show why this matters: these are exactly the environments where pressure, flammability, and process sensitivity make failure expensive.

The connection is not just technical, it is operational. In severe-duty systems, a heat exchanger is not only transferring energy; it is also protecting the plant from contamination, shutdown, or safety incidents. Kelvion’s product choices tell you which failures it considers unacceptable.

## Example
A hydrogen refuelling station and a carbon-capture plant both need thermal hardware that can handle high pressure and tight safety margins. In one case, `K°Bond` may be the right answer; in another, double-safety shell-and-tube gives operators a leak-detection buffer that is more valuable than maximum compactness.

## Analysis
Kelvion’s severe-duty design pattern is to add a second containment layer, use a bonding process that eliminates leak paths, or both. That pattern is consistent across product lines and shows up in the patent trail as well.
