# Plate Heat Exchangers

Kelvion treats plate heat exchangers as a broad family rather than a single product. Its umbrella page explains the basic principle: corrugated plates create alternating channels, hot and cold media flow through those channels, and the corrugation drives turbulence and heat transfer while keeping the fluids separated. The family includes brazed, gasketed, welded, and process-optimized designs, with countercurrent flow being the usual default and cross-flow or parallel flow appearing in specialized cases.

The important design distinction is not just construction, but operational envelope. Brazed plate heat exchangers are small and compact, suited to HVAC and refrigeration. Gasketed plate exchangers are modular and extendable, which makes them attractive when capacity or configuration needs change over time. Welded plate exchangers trade some modularity for high temperature, high pressure, and gas-tight operation. The product family therefore spans the full range from commodity building systems to severe-duty industrial service.

Kelvion’s own product pages reinforce that range with concrete numbers. The brazed series includes drinking-water-certified variants; the gasketed series includes a semi-welded heat pump design rated up to `64 bar(g)` on the welded side; the fully welded `K°Bloc` line emphasizes thermal transfer, robustness, and reduced cleaning/service burden. In other words, the family is organized around application fit, not just geometry.

## Key Points
- Plate exchangers use corrugated plates to create turbulent flow and high heat-transfer coefficients.
- Brazed, gasketed, and welded designs are the core segmentation axis.
- Brazed units optimize compactness; gasketed units optimize flexibility; welded units optimize pressure, temperature, and sealing.
- Plate exchangers show up in HVAC, refrigeration, district heating, food, marine, chemical, and oil-and-gas service.

## Example
A heat pump manufacturer choosing between a gasketed semi-welded plate exchanger and a fully welded plate exchanger is really choosing between serviceability and process severity. For a commercial industrial heat pump with high pressure on one side, Kelvion’s `HPL`/semi-welded concept gives high efficiency and service flexibility; for a harsh process loop with gas-tight requirements, `K°Bloc` or another welded design is the safer option.
