# Gasketed Plate Heat Exchanger

Kelvion’s gasketed plate exchangers are the serviceable, adaptable branch of the plate family. The product page emphasizes high efficiency, low operating cost, and flexibility, but the more important property is maintainability: gasketed plates can be reconfigured, expanded, or serviced over time. That is why this design is common in food, chemical, marine, district energy, and commercial heat-pump duty.

The page also highlights a semi-welded heat-pump variant (`HPL`) that combines a gasketed structure with a welded side that can tolerate much higher pressure on the critical circuit. That hybrid approach is important because it shows how Kelvion treats the gasketed family as an engineering platform, not as a fixed product. The same geometry can support different pressure and media profiles by changing how one side of the exchanger is built.

## Key Points
- Designed for flexibility, serviceability, and lower lifecycle cost.
- Highly suited to applications where capacity may need to grow or be reconfigured.
- The `HPL` semi-welded variant extends the family into higher-pressure heat-pump service.
- Often chosen when uptime and easy gasket replacement matter more than maximum compactness.

## Example
A district-heating operator may prefer a gasketed exchanger because the plant can grow, swap plates, and keep the unit in service over many years. If the same operator later needs to run a higher-pressure industrial heat pump loop, the semi-welded `HPL` version can bridge the gap without abandoning the gasketed platform entirely.
