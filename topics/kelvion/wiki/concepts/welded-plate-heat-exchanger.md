# Welded Plate Heat Exchanger

Kelvion’s welded plate exchangers are the severe-duty branch of the plate family. The product page emphasizes small size, strong thermal transfer coefficients, low investment cost, and robustness in high-temperature and high-pressure service. That makes welded units the natural choice when the exchanger has to behave more like a pressure-boundary component than a serviceable plate pack.

The flagship `K°Bloc` line adds decades of plate-pack welding experience and corrugation variants such as chevron, double-dimple, and New Performance Chevron. The important thing here is not only the thermal performance, but the reliability logic: gas-tight operation, lower fouling in some geometries, and a better fit for demanding liquids, temperatures, and pressures. Kelvion’s own description frames the family as sustainable because high recovery cuts energy use and cooling-water demand.

## Key Points
- Best suited to high pressure, high temperature, and gas-tight duty.
- `K°Bloc` is the main welded platform, with multiple corrugation styles.
- Good fit when cleaning and service intervals must be minimized.
- Thermal efficiency and robustness are the design priorities.

## Example
In a carbon-capture power project, a fully welded plate exchanger can sit in a process loop where leakage is unacceptable and thermal performance must stay high under severe conditions. That is why Kelvion’s carbon-capture order mentions fully welded plate packages alongside single-tube exchangers.
