# Heating Solutions

Kelvion’s heating-solution pages are really about heat recovery and preheating under demanding conditions. The core products are air preheaters, air-to-air heat exchangers, economizers, air dryers, and steam-related heating systems. The design language is consistent with the rest of the company: maximize thermal efficiency, keep pressure drop low, and tailor the exchanger geometry to the process envelope.

The most specific page is the air preheater. Kelvion describes finned-tube and plain-tube variants, materials ranging from steel to stainless steel and aluminium, and a hydraulic-friction advantage of roughly `3` to `10` times lower than a round-tube alternative. It also says the product line is relevant for green hydrogen, solar/wind/hydro, industrial cooling processes, and gas cleaning. The air-to-air heat exchanger pushes the same logic further by moving heat from exhaust air to incoming fresh air, with welded or glued designs and working experience up to `1000°C`.

What ties the family together is that heating is treated as a systems problem, not a product category. These exchangers recover waste heat, preheat combustion air, condition industrial air streams, and help plants avoid throwing away recoverable energy. That makes the family important in decarbonization contexts where thermal efficiency translates directly into lower fuel use and lower emissions.

## Key Points
- Heating solutions are really heat-recovery systems for industrial and energy processes.
- Air preheaters reduce hydraulic friction and improve heat transfer with finned or plain tube designs.
- Air-to-air exchangers can be welded or glued, with low- to high-temperature variants.
- The family is especially relevant to hydrogen, gas cleaning, district energy, and industrial process heating.

## Example
A waste-to-energy plant can use an air preheater to recover energy from exhaust gases, then use the recovered heat to improve combustion efficiency or support another process step. If the gas stream is dirtier or the temperature is higher, the operator may switch to a welded air-to-air design instead of a glued one. That is a textbook Kelvion tradeoff: the same heat-recovery problem solved at different duty levels.
