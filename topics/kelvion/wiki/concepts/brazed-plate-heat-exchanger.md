# Brazed Plate Heat Exchanger

Kelvion’s brazed plate exchangers are the compact end of the plate family. The product page emphasizes automated production, short lead time, and small footprint, which is exactly why brazed units show up in HVAC and refrigeration rather than in the most severe industrial duty. The series also includes drinking-water-oriented variants and certifications, which matters when the same thermal compactness has to be paired with sanitary or regulatory constraints.

The technical appeal is simple: brazing removes the need for service gaskets while preserving a plate-style high-heat-transfer geometry. That makes the units more compact and often faster to manufacture, but it also makes them less reconfigurable than gasketed exchangers. In other words, brazed exchangers are optimized for volume, compactness, and quick delivery, not for easy field modification.

## Key Points
- Compact, automated, and fast to manufacture.
- Best suited to HVAC and refrigeration where footprint matters.
- Some variants are drinking-water certified and aimed at low-charge applications.
- Less serviceable than gasketed designs, but more compact.

## Example
The Fraunhofer low-charge propane project used Kelvion’s GK108H brazed plate exchanger as part of a heat-pump test series. That is a good real-world fit: a brazed exchanger gives the compactness and low fill volume needed for a refrigerant-reduction strategy.
