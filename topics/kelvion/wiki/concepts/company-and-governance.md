# Company and Governance

Kelvion is a global heat-exchange company built around one idea: thermal management is a core enabler of industrial efficiency. The company positions itself as a developer and manufacturer of heat exchange solutions for data centres, hydrogen, heat pumps, marine systems, HVAC, refrigeration, food and beverage, and many industrial process segments. Its site copy emphasizes a global sales, service, and production network, which matters because most of its value is not just in a catalog of products, but in the ability to configure, deliver, service, and replace those systems internationally.

The governance story is more than corporate branding. Kelvion’s sustainability pages describe an ESG-led `R3` strategy, ISO 14001 environmental management, a zero-accident safety target, DEI policy, whistleblowing channel, mandatory compliance training, and supplier governance. The quality policy ties this together: management-level responsibility, legal compliance, continuous improvement, and energy-efficient procurement. The net effect is that Kelvion presents itself as an industrial supplier whose operating model is meant to be auditable and repeatable, not just technically capable.

The ownership and financing context also matters. External reporting shows that the business was carved out of GEA Heat Exchangers, renamed Kelvion in 2015, remained under Triton ownership, and in 2025 moved toward Apollo majority ownership while Triton kept a minority stake. Investor relations material also shows a 2025 bond issuance at `EUR 750,000,000` with maturity in 2032. That combination suggests a capital-intensive industrial platform that has to balance portfolio investment, restructuring, and long-cycle customer support.

## Key Points
- Kelvion’s core promise is thermal efficiency across high-value industrial applications, not commodity heat exchange.
- The company’s stated governance stack includes ISO 14001, compliance training, whistleblowing, DEI, and a zero-accident safety target.
- Its operating footprint is international, with sales/service/production sites across many countries.
- Ownership shifted from GEA to Triton, then to Apollo majority control in 2025, which frames the company’s recent restructuring.

## Example
A hyperscale data center project needs a supplier that can deliver a liquid cooling skid in weeks, support commissioning on site, and service the installation later in Europe and North America. Kelvion’s combination of global production, service locations, and QHSE/compliance posture is what makes that kind of project feasible. The technical product is only part of the sale; the real deliverable is a governed industrial supply chain.
