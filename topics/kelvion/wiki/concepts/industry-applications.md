# Industry Applications

Kelvion’s application pages are where the company turns product families into market stories. The portfolio spans data centres, HVAC-R, district heating and cooling, heat pumps, power and energy, green hydrogen, carbon capture, heavy and light industry, transportation, and related subsegments such as blockchain infrastructure and immersion cooling. The same hardware can be framed differently depending on the buyer: a dry cooler for a broadcasting unit, a plate exchanger for a heat pump, a liquid-cooling skid for a data center, or a shell-and-tube bundle for a process plant.

The detail that makes these pages valuable is the operational context. The liquid-cooling page says liquids can have up to `1200x` the heat capacity per unit volume of air and notes that modern GPUs/CPUs can exceed `400W` thermal design power. The BBC Springwatch case study says a `250kW` hydrogen power unit with a Kelvion dry air cooler saved about `60kg` of CO2 per hour, or `14,400 kg` over `20 days`. The Servex heat-pump story gives a concrete temperature target: hot water up to `80°C` even with ambient conditions down to `-20°C`.

That is the real pattern: Kelvion sells to market problems, not just industries. In data centres, the problem is density and uptime. In HVAC-R, it is seasonal efficiency and refrigerant choice. In carbon capture, it is thermal control under high energy intensity. In green hydrogen, it is electrolysis and downstream purification. In transportation, it is reliability under vibration, space, and ambient stress.

## Key Points
- Data-centre cooling is moving from air cooling toward liquid, immersion, and hybrid systems.
- Heat pumps, district energy, and carbon capture all depend on precise thermal management.
- Kelvion’s application pages often quantify the operating envelope, not just the hardware.
- The same product family can show up in multiple industries depending on the duty cycle.

## Example
A hyperscale AI data center may start with a liquid-to-liquid cooling loop for chip-level heat removal, add a dry cooler for heat rejection, and then use a service-backed operating model to keep the system online. If the same company later expands into immersion cooling for bitcoin mining, Kelvion’s thermal modules can still fit because the design problem is density, power, and heat transport, not a single named industry.
