GREEN HYDROGEN AS AN EFFICIENT ENERGY SOURCE
In the world of renewable energy, green hydrogen plays a key role, especially in Germany, where the production and manufacture of green hydrogen is being driven forward using advanced technologies. By using electrolysis processes that use renewable energy sources, water is split into hydrogen and oxygen. Leading technologies in this field include alkaline electrolysis (AEC) and proton exchange membrane electrolysis (PEMEC), both of which are known for their efficiency in the environmentally friendly production of hydrogen. Kelvion also contributes to sustainable energy production with its innovative heat exchange solutions.

Green Hydrogen Solutions
Kelvion: Efficiency in electrolysis through heat exchanger solutions
At the heart of green hydrogen production lies electrolysis - a process that requires not only precision but also sustainability. To ensure that electrolysis takes place under stable and efficient conditions, it is therefore important that all systems and equipment interacting with the process, especially the heat exchangers, are carefully selected and designed. Kelvion offers various heat exchanger solutions for thermal management in electrolysis systems.
Sustainable and innovative: Kelvion's solutions for green hydrogen production
Kelvion has a long-established history in the heat exchanger industry and will continue to support the sustainable and innovative development of green hydrogen in the future. In addition to heat exchangers for the core electrolysis system, our portfolio includes solutions for the upstream and downstream auxiliary processes in power-to-gas applications. We have also successfully supplied heat exchanger solutions for water treatment. Our transformer cooling systems are used worldwide. Both applications can be found in the upstream process of electrolyser power-to-gas plants. For downstream applications, we can draw on our expertise and experience in gas purification and compression. We would be happy to advise and support you in selecting the right heat exchanger technology and products for the production of green hydrogen as an energy source.
