# Servex and Kelvion collaborate on sustainable heat pump technology

Success stories
Servex and Kelvion collaborate on sustainable heat pump technology
As global efforts to reduce carbon emissions continue, Servex, a manufacturer of refrigeration and heat pump systems, remains committed to innovation with sustainability at its core. For more than 50 years, Servex has been a pioneer in refrigeration, air conditioning and air treatment solutions, and since 2014 has transitioned to using only natural refrigerants such as CO2 and propane. The company's mission fits perfectly with the global push for decarbonisation.
Their heat pump systems are designed to operate efficiently in extreme conditions, producing hot water at temperatures of up to 80°C, even when the ambient temperature drops to -20°C. Central to this achievement are Kelvion's custom-designed air coolers, developed over a decade of collaboration.
A decade of partnership and innovation
“The cooperation with Kelvion is great,” says Wil van den Kerkhof, Servex’s Managing Director. “We do a lot of development together, always striving to make products better. When we need something special that’s not on the market, Kelvion delivers.”
Kelvion’s R&D team worked closely with Servex to develop a customised gas cooler that not only met the technical demands of their heat pumps but also delivered best-in-class performance. The solution minimises defrosting and downtime, ensuring seamless operation even in challenging climates.
Technical excellence with a sustainable focus
Customised Kelvion air coolers are an integral part of Servex heat pump systems. Designed to meet the unique requirements of natural refrigerants, these components optimise performance and energy consumption. "Kelvion's solutions offer the best technical performance and cooling capacity compared to the competition," explains van den Kerkhof. "Their ability to reduce energy consumption is perfectly in line with our sustainability goals."
A shared vision for the future
Sustainability and efficient operation remain at the heart of Servex’s mission. By replacing traditional central heating systems with innovative heat pump solutions, Servex is driving decarbonisation across the building sector. Kelvion’s ability to deliver highly customised and sustainable components has been instrumental in this endeavour.
Reflecting on the partnership, Wil van den Kerkhof adds, “Kelvion’s technical expertise and commitment to developing bespoke solutions have been crucial to our success. I would recommend Kelvion to any company looking for innovative, customised solutions that are not readily available on the market.”
