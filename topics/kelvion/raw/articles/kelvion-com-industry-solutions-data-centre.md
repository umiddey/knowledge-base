Data Centre Solution
Basics of data centre cooling
Cooling data centres is a critical task that ensures the efficiency, security and service life of the server infrastructure. Adequate air conditioning of data centres not only protects against data loss due to overheating, but also makes a significant contribution to energy savings. At a time when data traffic and storage requirements are growing exponentially, optimising cooling systems is the order of the day.
When cooling data centres with mission-critical data, very large amounts of heat must be dissipated reliably and efficiently. This sensitive technology requires compliance with precisely defined temperature and humidity limits. If it is too dry, the sensitive computers can be damaged by electrostatic discharges. If the humidity is too high, on the other hand, there is a risk of corrosion.
Our effective cooling systems for your sensitive technology in the data centre
The temperatures in the data centre vary depending on the servers and the design of the cooling system. However, temperature fluctuations above the ASHRAE guideline values or beyond the manufacturer's specifications can have a massive impact on performance. As downtime in the data centre can have catastrophic consequences, the right measures should be taken to cool data centres. Efficient and reliable cooling systems are therefore absolutely essential.
Our range includes cooling components for a wide range of coolants or refrigerants - especially for demanding cooling tasks in data centres. We develop and manufacture recoolers, which we also adapt to local conditions according to customer requirements. Our portfolio includes standard V-coolers, adiabatic drycoolers, hybrid drycoolers and SuperXL units. The aim is always to extend the free cooling per year and therefore  the power consumption of data centres.
