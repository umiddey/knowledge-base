Functionality & importance of liquid-based cooling in modern data processing
The demands on data centres are constantly increasing, and with them the need for efficient and powerful cooling solutions. White Space Cooling Liquid Based represents the forefront of this development by using liquids for heat absorption, which have a much higher heat capacity than air. Compared with air-based cooling solutions, liquids have up to 1200 times more heat capacity per unit volume. This enables more efficient heat dissipation and makes it possible for the chips to work harder. Thanks to this technology, servers can be kept cool and reliable even under extreme load conditions.
The cooling capacity of liquids combined with a smaller footprint make Liquid-2-Liquid systems ideal for edge data centres. By equipping these systems with liquid cooling, it is possible to accommodate more computing power in a smaller space. The chips in powerful GPUs and CPUs also achieve a thermal design power of over 400 W. A large number of these server types make conventional air cooling units less practical and cost-effective. There are two main types of Liquid-2-Liquid cooling: direct to chip and immersion.
