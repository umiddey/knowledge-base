Air Cooled Condenser A-Frame
MASH System
The prime design for vacuum air-cooled condensers is based on MASH concept. Steam is brought to the condenser via a steam duct and a manifold. Steam flows down across 2 or 3 rows of finned tubes. Condensates are recovered inside bonnet header boxers connected to a hot water tank. Non-condensable go up through a deflegmation row for final condensation.
Answering most of API 661 (ISO 13706) and ASME VIII div 1 requirements
U stamped when required by end user
Optimised selection for plot plan restrictions using Groovy fins
Single Row System
The Single Row system consists of an aluminium coated steel flat tubes. Aluminium fins are brazed to the aluminium coated flat tubes. The solder filling the gap between the foot of the fins and the core tube provides excellent heat transfer properties.
Low Steam Side pressure drop for better vacuum and low condensate sub-cooling
Optimal use of the complete cooling surface as no-flow regions are avoided
Most resistant to freezing
A-Tube System
A-tube consists of an elliptical bare tube with rectangular carbon steel fins. Heat transfer is optimised by hot dip galvanization. The gap between the fin foot and the fin is filled completely with zinc to achieve excellent heat transfer properties.
Hot dip galvanising provides for outstanding corrosion protection
Suitable for vacuum operation as well as for pressure condensers subject to PED or ASME VIII div 1
